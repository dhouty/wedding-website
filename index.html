Frequently Asked Questions

What should I wear?
We recommend wearing cocktail or formal attire, including full- or midi-length dresses, jumpsuits, suits and ties, dress pants, etc. Please note that the ceremony and cocktail hour will be outdoors so choose your footwear accordingly. We suggest bringing a change of shoes to be more comfortable if you plan to walk around the resort before or after the wedding.
What will the weather be like in October in Southern California?
For our fellow Seattleites who are accustomed to trick-or-treating in the rain, please keep in mind that the weather in October tends to be much warmer in Southern California. In our experience going to Disneyland for Halloween, the weather tends to be in the mid-70s to low 80s. Since our wedding will take place in the evening, we recommend bringing a light jacket or sweater to wear as the sun starts to set and we move indoors for our air-conditioned reception.
What time should I arrive for the ceremony?
Our ceremony will begin at 5pm. Please plan to arrive 15 minutes early if you need assistance finding our ceremony venue or if you wish to park onsite at the resort.
Where is the wedding venue located?
Our wedding will take place at the Disneyland Hotel, which is located near the western entrance to the Downtown Disney District and has close proximity to Disneyland and California Adventure. The address for the Disneyland Hotel is 1150 West Magic Way, Anaheim, CA 92802.
How do I find the venues for each event on the day of the wedding?
Disney staff will escort our guests from the ceremony to the cocktail hour and from the cocktail hour to the reception. Additionally, our wedding party will know the locations of our wedding events and will be available to assist everyone.
Is there parking available at the hotel on the day of the wedding?
Complimentary self-parking will be available for our guests the day of the wedding an hour before our ceremony starts to an hour after our reception ends (4pm to 12:30am). To have your parking validated, tell the parking attendant you are visiting the resort for the wedding in honor of Rebecca Leslie and Daniel Houtsma. Valet services are available as well but parking fees will apply.
Can I bring my children to the wedding?
Although this is a Disney wedding and we love your little ones, we respectfully request no children under the age of 5 to give all our guests the opportunity to celebrate and relax. Thank you for understanding.
Can I bring a plus one?
Only guests named in your RSVP are invited to attend. Thank you for understanding.
Can I take pictures during the ceremony?
We have hired photographers and videographers to capture our wedding ceremony so that you can sit back, relax, and enjoy the moment with us. With that in mind, we kindly ask you to turn off all devices during our ceremony.
What kind of COVID-19 safety protocols are you following for the wedding?

Please be fully vaccinated for COVID-19 prior to attending our wedding. If you test positive for COVID-19 within ten days of our wedding or have any cold or flu-like symptoms, we kindly ask you to stay home. We will miss your presence at our wedding, but our guests' health and safety, including yours, is the highest priority.

Becky and Daniel plan to wear masks on the flight to and from California and encourage anyone traveling to the wedding to do the same. Our ceremony and cocktail hours will be hosted outdoors, and individually plated meals will be served at our reception. Currently, masks are not required at our wedding venue, but we completely respect your decision to wear one if you choose to do so.

Are Becky and Daniel planning to spend time in the parks?
Yes! Becky and Daniel will spend three or four days in Disneyland and California Adventure. If you are going to the parks as well, we would love to know your travel plans so we can ride one of the attractions with you!
Are there any other events happening the week of the wedding?

On Thursday, October 26, Becky and Daniel will attend the Oogie Boogie Bash, a Halloween party in California Adventure featuring the gambling man himself from The Nightmare Before Christmas. The first time we attended the Oogie Boogie Bash was the day Daniel proposed in Disneyland, and it is a very fond memory of the start of our engagement. A ticket to the Oogie Boogie Bash gives you access to California Adventure starting at 3pm and exclusive access after the party starts at 6pm with rides, trick-or-treating, a special parade, and meet-and-greets with your favorite Disney villains.

Tickets for the Oogie Boogie Bash will go on sale to the general public on Thursday, June 29 at 9am. The number of tickets available for each night of the party are limited. In past years, tickets for every night of the Oogie Boogie Bash have completely sold out within 24 hours. If you want to attend the party on October 26 with Becky and Daniel, we encourage you to purchase your tickets as soon as they become available!
